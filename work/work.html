Work

  • (1905)

    Theory of Special Relativity

    Albert Einstein made significant contributions to various fields of physics and theoretical science. His work is characterized by its profound impact on our understanding of the universe. Here are some of the key areas and contributions associated with Albert Einstein's work:

  • (1905)

    Photoelectric Effect

    n 1905, Einstein published his theory of special relativity, introducing the famous equation E=mc^2 (energy-mass equivalence). Special relativity transformed our understanding of space and time, showing that they are interconnected and not absolute. It also introduced the concept of time dilation, where time can pass differently for two observers moving relative to each other.

  • (1915)

    Theory of General Relativity

    Einstein explained the photoelectric effect, demonstrating that light has both wave-like and particle-like properties. His work on the photoelectric effect was instrumental in the development of quantum mechanics.

  • (1905)

    Brownian Motion

    In 1915, Einstein published his theory of general relativity, which provided a new understanding of gravity. General relativity proposed that massive objects, like planets and stars, warp the fabric of spacetime, causing gravitational attraction. It predicted the bending of light by gravity, which was confirmed during the 1919 solar eclipse, making Einstein a global scientific celebrity.

      Mass-Energy Equivalence

      Einstein's explanation of Brownian motion provided empirical evidence for the existence of atoms and molecules. He showed that the erratic motion of small particles suspended in a fluid could be explained by the random motion of molecules.

    • (1905)

      Quantum Mechanics:

      Einstein's equation, E=mc^2, demonstrated the equivalence of mass and energy, laying the foundation for nuclear physics. This equation became a fundamental principle in physics and played a crucial role in the development of nuclear energy.
