Albert Einstein: A Genius of the 20th Century

Albert Einstein, a name synonymous with genius, revolutionized our understanding of the universe and reshaped the course of modern physics. Born in Ulm, Germany, on March 14, 1879, Einstein's remarkable life and scientific contributions continue to captivate and inspire people worldwide. This essay delves into the life, work, and enduring impact of one of the most brilliant minds of the 20th century.

EDUCATION & CAREER

Early Life and Education

Einstein's early life was marked by both brilliance and nonconformity. His parents, Hermann and Pauline Einstein, were of modest means, and his early education at the Luitpold Gymnasium in Munich was marred by a clash with the rigid and authoritarian teaching style prevalent in Germany at the time. Despite his struggles with formal education, Einstein displayed an insatiable curiosity and a gift for mathematics and physics from a young age.

Albert Einstein, (born March 14, 1879, Ulm, Württemberg, Germany—died April 18, 1955, Princeton, New Jersey, U.S.), German-born physicist who developed the special and general theories of relativity and won the Nobel Prize for Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect
