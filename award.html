NOBEL PRIZE IN PHYSICS
1921

Albert Einstein was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which demonstrated the particle-like nature of light and provided important support for the theory of quantum mechanics.

COPLEY MEDAL
1925

The Royal Society of London awarded Einstein the Copley Medal in 1925 for his contributions to theoretical physics.

MATTEUCCI MEDAL
1923

The Matteucci Medal is an Italian scientific award, and Einstein received it in 1923 for his outstanding contributions to physics.

FRANKLIN MEDAL
1935

The Franklin Institute awarded Albert Einstein the Franklin Medal in 1935 for his work on the photoelectric effect.

GOLD MEDAL OF THE ROYAL ASTRONOMICAL SOCIETY
1926

Einstein was awarded the Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society in 1926 for his work on the theory of relativity.

MAX PLANCK MEDAL
1929

This medal is awarded by the German Physical Society (Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft) and was given to Einstein in 1929 for his significant contributions to theoretical physics.

TIME MAGAZINE'S PERSON OF THE CENTURY
1999

In 1999, Time magazine named Albert Einstein as the "Person of the Century" for his profound impact on science and the world.

SPECIAL THEORY OF RELATIVITY ON A COMMEMORATIVE STAMP
2005

In honor of the 100th anniversary of Einstein's "Annus Mirabilis" papers, the Swiss government issued a commemorative stamp featuring his famous equation, E=mc^2, in 2005.
